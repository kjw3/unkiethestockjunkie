What is a Screener?

A stock screener is a tool that helps you filter stocks based on specific criteria. Think of it like a search engine for stocks - you tell it what you're looking for, and it shows you companies that match.

Each screener below teaches you how a famous investor thinks about finding opportunities. Remember: these are for educational purposes only!

Buffett Screener

Find "wonderful companies at fair prices"

Warren Buffett's approach to value investing focuses on companies with durable competitive advantages, strong financials, and honest management.

Key Criteria:

  • Strong free cash flow
  • Low debt-to-equity ratio
  • Consistent ROE > 15%
  • Sustainable competitive advantages
  • Honest, capable management

Graham Screener

Find bargains with a "margin of safety"

Benjamin Graham, the father of value investing, taught Warren Buffett to buy stocks trading below their intrinsic value.

Key Criteria:

  • Low P/E ratio (< 15)
  • P/B ratio < 1.5
  • Dividend yield > 3%
  • Strong balance sheet
  • Current ratio > 2

Lynch Screener

Find growth at a reasonable price

Peter Lynch's "invest in what you know" philosophy helped him turn $18 million into $14 billion at Fidelity Magellan.

Key Criteria:

  • Earnings growth > 20%
  • Reasonable PEG ratio
  • Understandable business
  • Strong competitive position
  • Insider ownership

Icahn Screener

Find undervalued companies with hidden potential

Carl Icahn's activist approach seeks companies trading below their asset value that could benefit from change.

Key Criteria:

  • Trading below book value
  • Potential for restructuring
  • Strong cash position
  • Underperforming but fixable
  • Asset-rich operations
