        <div class="faq-section" id="screeners">
          <h2>Screeners</h2>
          
          <div class="faq-item">
            <button class="faq-question" aria-expanded="false">
              <span>What is a stock screener?</span>
              <span class="faq-icon">+</span>
            </button>
            <div class="faq-answer">
              <p>A stock screener is like a search engine for stocks. You tell it what criteria you're looking for (like "low debt" or "high growth"), and it shows you companies that match. Think of it like filtering products on Amazon, but for stocks!</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          
          <div class="faq-item">
            <button class="faq-question" aria-expanded="false">
              <span>Should I buy the stocks the screeners show?</span>
              <span class="faq-icon">+</span>
            </button>
            <div class="faq-answer">
              <p><strong>No!</strong> Our screeners are for educational purposes only. They show you what legendary investors look for, but they don't tell you to buy or sell anything. Always do your own research and consider talking to a financial advisor before investing real money.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          
          <div class="faq-item">
            <button class="faq-question" aria-expanded="false">
              <span>Why do you have different screeners for different investors?</span>
              <span class="faq-icon">+</span>
            </button>
            <div class="faq-answer">
              <p>Each famous investor has their own style! Buffett looks for wonderful companies at fair prices. Graham looks for bargains with a margin of safety. Lynch looks for growth companies you can understand. Icahn looks for underperforming companies with hidden value. Try them all and see which approach resonates with you!</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          
          <div class="faq-item">
            <button class="faq-question" aria-expanded="false">
              <span>How often are the screener results updated?</span>
              <span class="faq-icon">+</span>
            </button>
            <div class="faq-answer">
              <p>Our screeners use delayed data for educational purposes. Stock prices change constantly during market hours, so the examples shown are snapshots for learning. Always check current data before making any investment decisions.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
        </div>
        
        <div class="faq-section" id="investing-basics">
          <h2>Investing Basics</h2>
          
          <div class="faq-item">
            <button class="faq-question" aria-expanded="false">
              <span>How much money do I need to start investing?</span>
              <span class="faq-icon">+</span>
            </button>
            <div class="faq-answer">
              <p>You can start with very little! Many brokerages let you buy fractional shares, so you could start with just $1. But more importantly, start with knowledge first. Learn as much as you can before putting any money in the market.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          
          <div class="faq-item">
            <button class="faq-question" aria-expanded="false">
              <span>Can I lose money investing in stocks?</span>
              <span class="faq-icon">+</span>
            </button>
            <div class="faq-answer">
              <p>Yes, absolutely. Investing involves risk, and you can lose some or all of your investment. That's why we emphasize education and practice before you invest real money. The stock market goes up over the long term, but it can be volatile in the short term.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          
          <div class="faq-item">
            <button class="faq-question" aria-expanded="false">
              <span>What's the difference between a stock and a share?</span>
              <span class="faq-icon">+</span>
            </button>
            <div class="faq-answer">
              <p>These terms are often used interchangeably, but technically: "stock" refers to ownership in a company generally, while "share" refers to one unit of that ownership. You own "shares of stock" in a company. Think of it like a pizza - the whole pizza is the "stock," and each slice is a "share."</p>
            </div>
          </div>
          
          <div class="faq-item">
            <button class="faq-question" aria-expanded="false">
              <span>What's better: saving or investing?</span>
              <span class="faq-icon">+</span>
            </button>
            <div class="faq-answer">
              <p>Both! Save for short-term needs (emergency fund, upcoming purchases) and invest for long-term goals (retirement, college, wealth building). Savings keep your money safe but grow slowly. Investing has more risk but historically returns more over time.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
        </div>

<script>
// FAQ Accordion functionality
document.addEventListener('DOMContentLoaded', function() {
  const faqQuestions = document.querySelectorAll('.faq-question');
  
  faqQuestions.forEach(question => {
    question.addEventListener('click', function() {
      const isExpanded = this.getAttribute('aria-expanded') === 'true';
      const answer = this.nextElementSibling;
      
      // Close all other questions
      faqQuestions.forEach(q => {
        if (q !== this) {
          q.setAttribute('aria-expanded', 'false');
          q.nextElementSibling.classList.remove('active');
        }
      });
      
      // Toggle current question
      this.setAttribute('aria-expanded', !isExpanded);
      answer.classList.toggle('active');
    });
  });
});
</script>
