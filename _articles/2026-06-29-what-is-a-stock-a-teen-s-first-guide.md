**Difficulty: Beginner**

**What Is It?**
---------------

So, what is a stock? Simply put, a stock is a tiny piece of a company. When you buy a stock, you're essentially buying a small portion of that company's ownership. Think of it like owning a slice of pizza. If you own a slice, you have a claim to a part of the whole pizza. Similarly, when you own a stock, you have a claim to a part of the company's assets and profits.

**Why Should Teens Care?**
-------------------------

You might be wondering why stocks matter to you. Well, here's the thing: investing in stocks can be a powerful way to grow your money over time. Think about it like this: if you start saving and investing early, you can potentially earn thousands of dollars in interest and dividends by the time you're ready to go to college or start your career. That's a pretty cool incentive to learn more about stocks!

**Key Concepts**
----------------

Here are some key concepts to understand when it comes to stocks:

* **Shares**: A share is a single unit of a company's stock. When you buy a stock, you're buying a certain number of shares.
* **Equity**: Equity refers to the value of the shares you own. If the company does well, the value of your shares might go up.
* **Dividends**: Dividends are payments made by the company to its shareholders. If you own shares, you might be eligible to receive dividend payments.
* **Market**: The market refers to the place where stocks are bought and sold. Think of it like a big store where people trade shares.

**Real-World Examples**
----------------------

Let's look at a real-world example. Imagine you own 10 shares of Apple stock. If Apple releases a new iPhone and the company's profits go up, the value of your shares might increase. You could then sell your shares for a profit or hold onto them and potentially earn more dividends.

Another example is Amazon. If you had invested in Amazon stock 10 years ago, your investment would be worth a lot more today. That's because Amazon has grown rapidly and become one of the world's largest companies.
