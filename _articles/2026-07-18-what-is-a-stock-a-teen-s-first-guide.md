**What is a Stock? A Teen's First Guide**
=====================================================

**Difficulty: Beginner**
**Tags: stocks, basics, fundamentals, beginner**

**Introduction**
---------------

Hey there, young investor! Are you curious about the world of stocks and investing? Maybe you've heard your parents or friends talking about the stock market, but you're not sure what it's all about. Well, you're in the right place! In this article, we'll break down the basics of stocks and why they matter to you, even if you're just a teenager.

**What Is It?**
--------------

So, what is a stock? Simply put, a stock represents ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you're essentially buying a tiny piece of that company. Think of it like owning a small part of your favorite video game company, like Epic Games (the creators of Fortnite). If the company does well and makes more money, the value of your stock might go up!

**Why Should Teens Care?**
-------------------------

You might be wondering why you should care about stocks if you're still in school. Well, here's the thing: investing in stocks can be a powerful way to grow your money over time. Even if you're not planning to invest right now, understanding how stocks work can help you make informed decisions about your money in the future. Plus, it's never too early to start learning about personal finance and investing!

**Key Concepts**
----------------

Here are some key concepts to understand when it comes to stocks:

* **Stock exchange**: A place where people buy and sell stocks, like the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) or NASDAQ.
* **Stock price**: The current price of a single share of stock.
* **Share**: A single unit of ownership in a company.
* **Portfolio**: A collection of stocks and other investments that you own.
* **Dividend**: A payment made by a company to its shareholders, usually quarterly or annually.

**Real-World Examples**
----------------------

Let's look at a real-world example. Imagine you bought 10 shares of Apple stock (AAPL) last year for $100 each. Today, the stock price is $150 per share. That means your investment is now worth $1,500! You could sell your shares and make a profit of $500.

Another example is Coca-Cola (KO). The company has been paying dividends to its shareholders for over 100 years. If you owned 100 shares of Coca-Cola stock, you could receive a quarterly dividend payment of around $40.

**Key Takeaways**
-----------------

Here are the main lessons from this article:
