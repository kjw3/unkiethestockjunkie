=====================================================

**Difficulty: Beginner**

**What Is It?**
----------------

A stock, also known as equity, is a type of security that represents ownership in a company. Think of it like owning a small piece of your favorite pizza joint. When you buy a stock, you're essentially buying a tiny piece of that company's assets and profits.

Imagine you own a lemonade stand with your friends. You all contribute money to make it happen, and in return, you get a share of the profits. If the lemonade stand makes more money, the value of your share increases. That's basically what a stock is – a share of a company's success (or failure).

**Why Should Teens Care?**
---------------------------

So, why should you care about stocks? Well, investing in the stock market can be a great way to grow your money over time. It's like planting a seed in the ground and watching it grow into a tree. With patience and the right knowledge, you can build wealth and achieve your long-term financial goals.

Plus, learning about stocks can help you understand the business world and how companies work. It's like being part of a secret club where you get to learn about the inner workings of your favorite companies.

**Key Concepts**
-----------------

Here are some key concepts to understand:

* **Stock exchange**: A platform where stocks are bought and sold, like the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) or NASDAQ.
* **Stock price**: The current market price of a stock, which can fluctuate based on supply and demand.
* **Dividends**: Payments made by a company to its shareholders, usually quarterly or annually.
* **Portfolio**: A collection of stocks and other investments owned by an individual or institution.

**Real-World Examples**
------------------------

Let's look at some real-world examples:

* **Apple Inc. (AAPL)**: Imagine you bought Apple stock in 2001, when the iPhone didn't even exist. Today, your investment would be worth many times more, thanks to Apple's innovative products and growing profits.
* **GameStop Corp. (GME)**: In 2020, GameStop's stock price surged due to a surge in online gaming and a successful turnaround strategy. If you had invested in GameStop, you would have seen a significant return on your investment.
